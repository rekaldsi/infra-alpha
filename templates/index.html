📐 VWAP Bounce Setups — How It Works

Frank's strategy, explained from scratch

What is VWAP?

VWAP stands for Volume Weighted Average Price. It's the average price a stock has traded at throughout the day, weighted by how much volume traded at each price. Institutions — banks, hedge funds, big money — use VWAP as their benchmark. They buy below it and sell above it. That's why it acts as a magnet.

Think of VWAP as the "fair value" line for the day. Above it = bulls in control. Below it = bears in control.

What is a VWAP Bounce Setup?

A VWAP bounce happens when a stock:

  1. Is trading above VWAP (uptrend, bullish bias)
  2. Pulls back toward VWAP (sellers take profit, price dips)
  3. Touches or comes close to VWAP and holds (buyers defend the level)
  4. Bounces back up with a surge in volume (confirmation that buyers are back)

That bounce is your entry signal. You're buying where the institutions are buying, with a clear line in the sand for your stop.

🔥 HIGH vs ⚡ MEDIUM — What the Grades Mean

🔥 HIGH — Volume spike is 3x or more above average. Strong institutional buying. Highest conviction entry. Act fast — these don't last long.

⚡ MEDIUM — Volume spike is 1.5x–3x above average. Setup is valid but less explosive. Still worth taking, just size accordingly.

How to Trade It — Step by Step

  1. Wait for the setup to appear in the scanner (🔥 or ⚡ badge on the card)
  2. Click the stock card → opens TradingView. Switch to 5-minute candles.
  3. Confirm visually: price pulled back near VWAP, then bounced with a strong green candle and volume bar
  4. Enter on the bounce candle's close — don't chase if it's already run 2–3% from VWAP
  5. Set your stop just below VWAP (if it loses VWAP and can't reclaim in 1–2 candles, the setup failed)
  6. Target: previous high of day, or scale out in thirds as it moves in your favor

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Chasing after a big move — if price is already 4%+ above VWAP, you missed the setup. Wait for the next pullback.
  • Ignoring volume — a bounce without volume is weak. Institutions aren't buying. Skip it.
  • Not having a stop — VWAP is your line. Below it = exit. No exceptions.
  • Trading against the market — check the macro bar. If SPY is red and VIX is spiking, VWAP setups fail more often. Sit on hands.

Best Conditions for This Strategy

  • SPY is green or flat (bullish macro backdrop)
  • VIX below 22 (low fear = cleaner moves)
  • Stock has a catalyst or sector tailwind (e.g. earnings beat, sector rotation into AI infra)
  • Setup fires between 9:45–11:30 AM or 1:30–3:00 PM CST (highest institutional activity)
  • First VWAP test of the day is usually the strongest

⚠️ Not financial advice. VWAP bounce setups are a momentum strategy with real risk. Always use a stop loss. Never risk more than you're comfortable losing on a single trade. Past setups don't guarantee future results.
